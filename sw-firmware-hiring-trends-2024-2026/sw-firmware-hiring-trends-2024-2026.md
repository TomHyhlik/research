# Software & Firmware Developer Hiring Trends: 2024–Early 2026

**Summary:** This report examines software and firmware/embedded developer job market trends across the USA, Europe (EU/UK), and China from Q1 2024 through early Q1 2026, covering job posting volumes, senior vs. junior hiring disparities, regional breakdowns, and the key economic and technological forces shaping each market — with particular focus on AI's growing displacement of entry-level roles.

---

## 1. Global Overview

The software developer job market between 2024 and early 2026 is defined by a structural split: overall volumes remain well below the 2021–2022 hiring peaks, yet pockets of strong demand persist in AI/ML, cloud, cybersecurity, and embedded/firmware engineering. The defining macro force is generative AI, which is simultaneously creating new roles and suppressing demand for junior and mid-level generalist developers.

### Key global figures

| Metric | Value | Period |
|---|---|---|
| Global tech layoffs | ~245,000 jobs cut | Full year 2025 |
| 2026 YTD tech layoffs | ~60,000 (682/day) | Jan–Mar 2026 |
| AI-attributed layoffs (2025) | ~55,000 in USA alone (<8% cited AI) | 2025 |
| AI-attributed layoffs (2026 YTD) | ~9,238 of 45,363 confirmed (20.4%) | Early 2026 |
| Software dev job postings vs. 2022 peak | Down up to 90% at some companies | 2024 |
| Software dev job postings vs. 2020 | Down >33% | 2024 |
| Software engineering overall employment | 8.1% YoY growth (total stock) | 2025 |
| New software engineering job postings | -15% vs. same period prior year | Jan–Feb 2026 |

The divergence between headline employment growth (+8.1% stock, driven by retention) and new posting decline (-15%) reflects a market where fewer net new roles are being opened, but existing roles remain stable.

## 2. Senior vs. Junior Hiring Trends

This is arguably the most pronounced structural shift in the 2024–2026 period.

### Entry-level collapse

- Entry-level postings dropped **60%** between 2022 and 2024.
- In 2024, entry-level tech hiring declined a further **25% year-over-year**.
- Entry-level positions (P1/P2 job levels) saw a **73% decrease** in hiring rates over the year prior to early 2026.
- Employment among software developers aged 22–25 fell nearly **20%** from its late-2022 peak (Stanford University data).
- New graduates now account for just **7%** of hires at Big Tech firms, down **25%** from 2023.
- Google and Meta are hiring approximately **50%** fewer new graduates compared to 2021.
- Salesforce announced a halt to junior hiring for 2025 entirely.
- Unemployment rate for recent US computer engineering graduates: **7.5%**; computer science graduates: **6.1%**.

### Senior and mid-level resilience

- Postings for senior and manager-level tech jobs are down only **19%** from pre-pandemic levels.
- Standard and junior-level tech job postings are down **34%** from pre-pandemic levels — nearly double the senior decline.
- Mid-level and senior engineers are consistently favored over juniors in the current "experience-first" hiring environment.
- **54%** of engineering leaders surveyed (LeadDev, 2025) plan to hire fewer junior engineers due to AI copilots enabling seniors to handle greater workloads.

### AI as the primary driver

- **65%** of developers use AI coding tools at least weekly (Stack Overflow 2025 Developer Survey, 49,000+ respondents).
- AI tools now write an estimated **41%** of all code.
- Teams using AI tools report producing **40–55%** more code per sprint, effectively meaning a team of 10 with AI tools can match the output of 15 without.
- **37%** of employers say they would prefer to deploy AI rather than hire a recent graduate.
- Long-term risk: a sustained reduction in junior hiring from 2024–2026 implies a proportional shortage of senior candidates by 2031–2036.

## 3. United States

### Employment base

- Software developers held approximately **1.7 million jobs** in the USA as of mid-2024 (BLS, May 2024).
- Median annual wage: **$133,080** (May 2024).
- BLS 10-year projection: **+15% employment growth** from 2024 to 2034 (much faster than average), with approximately **129,200** job openings per year projected on average over the decade.

### Job posting volume trends (quarterly context)

- Software engineer job postings are up approximately **11% year-over-year** as of late 2024/early 2025, driven by Big Tech rebounds (Citadel Securities analysis).
- However, overall hiring is running approximately **20% below pre-pandemic (July 2019) baseline levels** on LinkedIn.
- Entry-level postings saw a **47% growth** in unique listings (71,184 listings for 0–3 years experience) between October 2023 and November 2024 — a brief bright spot, but still far below 2021–2022 peak levels.

### Quarterly summary

| Quarter | Trend | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Q1 2024 | Continued decline from 2023 peak layoffs | Post-FAANG layoff hangover; selective hiring |
| Q2 2024 | Stabilization; entry-level uptick | 47% entry-level listing growth over 12-month window begins |
| Q3–Q4 2024 | Recovery signs; Big Tech accelerating | 11% YoY postings growth; Big Tech hiring faster than 2022–2023 |
| Q1 2025 | Tech hiring 0.3% YoY growth; cautious | Month-over-month +10.6% but YoY barely positive |
| Q2–Q3 2025 | AI roles dominant; generalist roles soft | AI/ML postings up 34% YoY; entry-level P1/P2 down 73% |
| Q4 2025 | Tech layoffs continue; 245,000 cut in full year | Selective senior hiring; junior freeze widespread |
| Q1 2026 | New posting volumes -15% vs. Q1 2025 | AI layoff attribution rises to 20.4% of cuts |

### Drivers

- **AI productivity tools** suppressing need for headcount expansion, especially at junior levels.
- **Big Tech consolidation**: after 2022–2023 layoffs, companies now operate leaner and maintain that posture.
- **AI-specific roles booming**: LinkedIn data shows +34% YoY in AI/ML engineering postings as of March 2026.
- **San Francisco Bay Area** remains the epicenter of AI job creation, concentrating opportunities geographically.

## 4. Europe (EU & UK)

### EU employment base

- The EU employed over **10 million ICT specialists** in 2024 (approx. **5.1%** of total EU employment), having doubled from 2014 levels.
- EU "Digital Decade" target: **20 million digital experts** by 2030 — requiring nearly 10 million more workers in 6 years from 2024.
- **57%** of EU firms report inability to find qualified tech staff.
- **35% decline** in junior tech positions across major EU economies in 2024 (LinkedIn/Indeed/EURES).

### UK

- Net tech employment: estimated **2,179,005 workers** in 2024 (+0.8% vs. prior year).
- Tech employment projected to grow **+1.4%** in 2025.
- UK tech industry estimated at **1.8 million employees** as of 2025 (5.4% of total workforce).
- **73,000+** tech job vacancies advertised in Q1 2024; software engineers topped the list.
- Software developer re-entered UK's **top-5 most in-demand roles** in 2025.
- Advertised vacancies in May 2025: **858,465** (+0.49% vs. May 2024), third consecutive month of YoY vacancy growth.
- Median tech salary: **£49,410** (52% above national median across all occupations).
- Mid-level developer salaries rose **12% YoY**, averaging £65,000–£85,000.
- 46 tech unicorns as of 2024, making the UK the dominant European tech hub.

### Germany

- **24% decrease** in IT job listings since January 2024 — among the steeper EU contractions.
- Despite this, over **770,000** job vacancies exist in Germany's IT sector.
- Ratio: **3.1 job openings per available tech talent** (indicating persistent talent scarcity despite posting decline).
- Tech industry projected to grow **22.5%** in 2024, driving continued demand.
- Average embedded software engineer salary: **€66,500/year** (range: €56,850–€78,250).

### France

- **2.5 job openings per available software engineer**.
- Tech job openings projected to grow **25%** by 2027.
- French tech market projected to reach **€70 billion** at a 5.8% growth rate.
- Approximately **500,000** tech job openings expected.

### EU-wide entry-level situation

- **35% decline** in junior tech positions across major EU economies in 2024.
- The Netherlands saw an almost **40% drop** in entry-level developer roles.
- Germany's Mittelstand companies increasingly opting for automation or outsourcing over junior hires.
- Job postings mentioning generative AI jumped **80% year-over-year** (EU-wide).

### Summary: Europe direction

| Region | Overall Direction | Entry-Level | Senior/Specialist | AI/ML |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| UK | Flat to slight growth | Declining | Stable/growing | Booming |
| Germany | Declining postings; talent scarcity | Declining | Constrained supply | Growing |
| France | Growing demand | Challenging | Moderate growth | Booming |
| Broader EU | Mixed; persistent skills gap | -35% YoY | Stable/scarce | +80% YoY postings |

## 5. China

### Big Tech restructuring (2022–2025)

China's large tech employers underwent major workforce reductions:

| Company | Peak Workforce | Reduced To | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Alibaba | 254,941 (2022) | 204,891 (2024) → ~124,320 (March 2025) | Sharp 2025 drop partly reflects subsidiary divestitures |
| Baidu | ~45,500 (peak) | 35,900 (2024) | 21.1% staff reduction |
| Tencent | — | 10,000+ workers cut in recent years | Ongoing restructuring |

### Overall hiring conditions

- **42%** of Chinese businesses expect headcount increases in 2025.
- **60%** of organisations faced moderate to extreme skill shortages in 2024.
- Over **73%** of 2024 graduates preferred government or state-owned enterprise roles over private tech firms — a significant cultural shift driven by Big Tech instability and layoffs.
- China's broader economic slowdown and government crackdowns on private tech (2021–2023) created lasting hiring caution at large private firms.

### AI hiring boom (2025–2026)

Despite legacy Big Tech cuts, AI-specific hiring is surging:

- AI talent demand outpaced supply **3:1** in Q1 2025.
- AI sector job seekers surged **33.4%** in the first week after Chinese New Year 2025.
- Algorithm engineer postings grew **46.8% YoY**; machine learning roles **40.1% YoY** (February 2025).
- Average AI role salaries: **>20,000 yuan/month** (~$2,750 USD/month).
- DeepSeek's January 2026 model releases triggered a nationwide AI hiring scramble across Alibaba, Tencent, ByteDance, Huawei, and hundreds of smaller firms.
- DeepSeek specifically offers 14-month pay; deep learning researchers can earn up to **1.54 million yuan/year** (~$211,000 USD).
- As of March 2026: DeepSeek posted 17 new agentic AI job openings, signaling continued aggressive recruitment.

### Semiconductor and strategic tech

- China's "AI 2030" and semiconductor self-sufficiency initiatives are driving specialized hiring.
- Shein: hiring **1,000** engineers for real-time trend algorithms.
- Pony.ai: expanding to 15 cities seeking sensor fusion specialists.
- DeepRoute.ai: recruiting **500+** engineers for Level 5 autonomous trucking.
- 43,000+ software developer positions listed on LinkedIn for China as of late 2025.

### Summary: China direction

China's market is bifurcated: legacy Big Tech roles are shrinking as large platforms plateau and restructure, while AI, autonomous systems, and semiconductor roles are in aggressive expansion. The net talent balance favors engineering professionals with AI/ML skills, but traditional software generalist roles at large private tech firms remain under pressure.

## 6. Firmware & Embedded Systems: A Distinct Trajectory

Firmware and embedded engineering shows a markedly different pattern from general software development — characterized by persistent and worsening talent shortage rather than the oversupply seen in web/app development.

### USA numbers

- **99,014** Firmware Engineers employed in the United States (Zippia).
- **171,613** Embedded Software Engineers employed in the United States.
- Projected demand growth: **21%** from 2018 to 2028 (measured from Zippia baseline).
- Salary ranges: $105,000–$165,651/year for embedded software; average $92,522 for firmware engineers (PayScale, 2026).
- Salary range for IoT/embedded roles on ZipRecruiter: **$111,000–$215,000**.

### Europe numbers

- EU embedded systems market: projected to reach significant scale by 2034, growing at CAGR of 6.5% (to $137B globally by 2027).
- Semiconductor dominates EU embedded job postings: **33%** of all embedded openings.
- Automotive follows at **26%** of EU embedded openings.
- Average remote embedded software engineer salary in Europe: **€52,723/year**.
- Germany average: **€66,500/year** for embedded software engineers.

### The shortage problem

- **80%** of embedded engineering job postings go unfilled for months or years (Runtime Recruitment).
- **65%** of 200 surveyed embedded systems companies struggle to fill IoT, microcontroller, and embedded software roles.
- **30%** of organizations in 2025 reported shortage of embedded security architects specifically.
- The EU faces a significant deficit of embedded and firmware engineers by 2025, with Germany, France, and Italy accounting for the majority of unmet demand.

### Why embedded/firmware diverges from general software

1. **AI coding tools provide limited help**: Embedded work requires deep hardware knowledge, C/C++ on constrained systems, RTOS, bootloaders, and physical debugging — areas where AI code generators are less effective than in web development.
2. **Low graduate pipeline**: Students gravitate toward higher-visibility, higher-starting-salary web and mobile development, leaving embedded chronically underpopulated.
3. **Rising complexity**: Automotive (AUTOSAR, functional safety ISO 26262), medical devices, IoT security, and edge AI all increasing the skillset demands.
4. **Growth sectors driving demand**: EVs, autonomous vehicles, ADAS, Industry 4.0, medical IoT, smart home devices all require embedded/firmware talent.
5. **Rust adoption**: Growing employer demand for Rust as a safer alternative to C in firmware is creating additional skills gaps.

### In-demand skills (firmware/embedded, 2025)

- C and C++ (still dominant)
- Real-time operating systems (FreeRTOS, Zephyr, QNX)
- Hardware abstraction layers, device drivers, bootloaders
- Secure boot, firmware update (OTA) mechanisms
- Edge AI integration (TensorFlow Lite, ONNX on microcontrollers)
- Communication protocols: CAN, I2C, SPI, UART, BLE, MQTT
- Rust (growing rapidly)
- Functional safety standards (ISO 26262, IEC 61508, FDA for medical)

## 7. Key Drivers Summary

### AI impact (most significant factor)

- AI is the primary structural driver suppressing junior/entry-level hiring globally.
- AI coding tools are reducing per-developer output cost for generalist tasks.
- Simultaneously, AI is creating entirely new engineering roles (ML engineer, AI infrastructure, LLM fine-tuning) that command premium salaries.
- By early 2026, **20.4%** of confirmed layoffs cite AI/automation as a direct cause — up from less than 8% in 2025.

### Economic conditions

- USA: Cautious but recovering; Big Tech hiring below pandemic peaks but recovering from 2023 trough. Fed rate environment and macro uncertainty keep hiring selective.
- Europe: Weak hiring activity broadly; UK outperforms continent; Germany hit by manufacturing sector slowdown compounding IT budget pressure.
- China: Domestic economic slowdown + government regulation of private tech (2021–2023 crackdown aftermath) suppressing legacy hiring; AI strategy investments bucking the trend.

### Structural factors

- Post-pandemic hiring correction: The 2021–2022 "overhiring" era created a prolonged correction through 2024–2025.
- Remote work normalization: US developers commanding premium, European and Chinese talent increasing cross-border availability.
- Semiconductor strategic investments: USA (CHIPS Act), EU (European Chips Act), China (national AI/semiconductor plans) creating specialized embedded/hardware hiring booms distinct from software generalist trends.
