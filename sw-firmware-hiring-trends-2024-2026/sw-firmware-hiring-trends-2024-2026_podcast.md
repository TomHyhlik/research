# Software & Firmware Developer Hiring Trends: 2024–Early 2026

**Summary:** This report examines software and firmware/embedded developer job market trends across the USA, Europe (EU/UK), and China from Q1 2024 through early Q1 2026, covering job posting volumes, senior vs. junior hiring disparities, regional breakdowns, and the key economic and technological forces shaping each market — with particular focus on AI's growing displacement of entry-level roles.

---

## 1. Global Overview

The software developer job market between 2024 and early 2026 is defined by a structural split: overall volumes remain well below the 2021 through 2022 hiring peaks, yet pockets of strong demand persist in AI and machine learning, cloud, cybersecurity, and embedded and firmware engineering. The defining macro force is generative AI, which is simultaneously creating new roles and suppressing demand for junior and mid-level generalist developers.

Here are the key global figures. In the full year 2025, approximately 245,000 tech jobs were cut globally. In the first quarter of 2026 year-to-date, roughly 60,000 more have been cut, running at 682 people per day. In the USA alone, about 55,000 layoffs in 2025 were attributed to AI. By early 2026, that AI attribution rate has jumped to 20.4 percent of confirmed cuts, up from less than 8 percent in 2025. Software developer job postings are down more than 33 percent compared to 2020 levels, and compared to the 2022 peak at some companies, down by as much as 90 percent. On the flip side, total software engineering employment as a stock is up 8.1 percent year-over-year in 2025, but that reflects retained workers, not new hiring — because new posting volumes are actually down 15 percent in January and February 2026 compared to the same period in 2025.

---

## 2. Senior vs. Junior Hiring Trends

This is arguably the most dramatic structural shift in the 2024 through 2026 period.

Starting with entry-level. Between 2022 and 2024, entry-level postings dropped 60 percent. In 2024 alone, entry-level tech hiring declined a further 25 percent year-over-year. Entry-level, or what companies call P1 and P2 job levels, saw a staggering 73 percent decrease in hiring rates over the year prior to early 2026. Employment among software developers aged 22 to 25 fell nearly 20 percent from its late-2022 peak, according to Stanford University data. New graduates now account for just 7 percent of hires at Big Tech firms — down 25 percent from 2023. Google and Meta are hiring approximately 50 percent fewer new graduates compared to 2021. Salesforce halted junior hiring entirely for 2025. And the unemployment rate for recent US computer engineering graduates is 7.5 percent, while computer science graduates face 6.1 percent unemployment.

By contrast, senior and mid-level roles are holding up much better. Postings for senior and manager-level tech jobs are only down 19 percent from pre-pandemic levels, versus 34 percent for junior titles. Mid-level and senior engineers are consistently preferred in what hiring managers are calling an "experience-first" market.

AI is the primary driver of this split. 65 percent of developers are now using AI coding tools at least weekly, according to the 2025 Stack Overflow Developer Survey of 49,000 respondents. These tools now write an estimated 41 percent of all code. Teams using AI tools report 40 to 55 percent more code output per sprint, effectively meaning a team of 10 with AI can match the output of 15 without. And 54 percent of engineering leaders say they plan to hire fewer juniors because AI copilots let seniors handle more. 37 percent of employers say they'd rather deploy AI than hire a recent graduate.

There is a long-term risk worth noting: a sustained reduction in junior hiring from 2024 through 2026 implies a proportional shortage of senior candidates by 2031 through 2036 — but that reckoning is years away.

## 3. United States

The USA's software developer employment base stands at approximately 1.7 million jobs as of mid-2024 according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. The median annual wage is $133,080 as of May 2024. The 10-year projection shows plus-15 percent employment growth from 2024 to 2034 — much faster than average — with about 129,200 job openings per year projected on average over the decade.

For job posting volumes quarter by quarter: In Q1 2024, the market was still in a post-FAANG layoff hangover with continued decline from 2023 peaks. Q2 2024 saw stabilization and a brief entry-level uptick — that 47 percent growth in unique entry-level listings over the following 12-month window. Q3 and Q4 2024 brought recovery signs, with Big Tech accelerating hiring and software engineer postings up 11 percent year-over-year according to Citadel Securities analysis. Q1 2025 saw tech hiring grow just 0.3 percent year-over-year, with a 10.6 percent month-over-month bounce but overall hiring running 20 percent below pre-pandemic July 2019 baseline levels. Q2 and Q3 2025 showed AI roles dominant while generalist roles softened — AI and machine learning postings up 34 percent year-over-year but P1 and P2 levels down 73 percent. Q4 2025 saw the full-year toll reach 245,000 tech layoffs. And in Q1 2026, new posting volumes are 15 percent below Q1 2025, with AI attribution for layoffs rising to over 20 percent.

The key drivers in the US are AI productivity tools suppressing the need for headcount expansion — especially at junior levels — a Big Tech posture of operating deliberately leaner after 2022 and 2023 overcorrection layoffs, a booming AI-specific job market with LinkedIn showing plus-34 percent year-over-year in AI and ML engineering postings as of March 2026, and the San Francisco Bay Area remaining the geographic epicenter of that AI job creation.

## 4. Europe — EU and UK

Starting with the European Union overall. The EU employed over 10 million ICT specialists in 2024, representing roughly 5.1 percent of total EU employment — double the 2014 figure. The EU's Digital Decade target calls for 20 million digital experts by 2030, meaning the bloc needs to add nearly 10 million more tech workers in just 6 years from 2024. Today, 57 percent of EU firms report they simply cannot find qualified tech staff. Junior tech positions declined 35 percent across major EU economies in 2024. Job postings mentioning generative AI jumped 80 percent year-over-year across the EU.

For the United Kingdom specifically: net tech employment reached an estimated 2,179,005 workers in 2024, up 0.8 percent year-over-year, and projected to grow 1.4 percent in 2025. The broader UK tech industry estimates 1.8 million employees — 5.4 percent of the total workforce. In Q1 2024, over 73,000 tech vacancies were advertised with software engineers at the top. In May 2025, total advertised vacancies of 858,465 were up 0.49 percent versus May 2024 — the third consecutive month of year-on-year growth. Software developer has re-entered the UK's top five most in-demand roles. Median tech salary is £49,410 — 52 percent above the national median. Mid-level developer salaries rose 12 percent year-over-year, averaging between £65,000 and £85,000.

Germany's picture is mixed. IT job listings have declined 24 percent since January 2024. Yet the ratio of job openings to available talent stands at 3.1 to 1, indicating that despite declining postings, talent scarcity remains severe. Over 770,000 tech job vacancies exist. The tech industry projects 22.5 percent growth in 2024, and the average embedded software engineer earns €66,500 per year.

France shows a more optimistic picture. There are 2.5 job openings per available software engineer. Tech job openings are projected to grow 25 percent by 2027. The French tech market is targeting €70 billion in size at a 5.8 percent growth rate, with around 500,000 job openings expected.

The Netherlands saw an almost 40 percent drop in entry-level developer roles in 2024. Germany's Mittelstand companies — the mid-size industrial backbone — are increasingly choosing automation or outsourcing over hiring junior developers.

## 5. China

China's story is a tale of two markets. First, the legacy Big Tech restructuring. Alibaba's full-time workforce fell from 254,941 in 2022 to 204,891 in 2024, and further to approximately 124,320 by March 2025 — though that sharp 2025 decline partly reflects subsidiary divestitures. Baidu cut its staff by 21.1 percent, ending 2024 with 35,900 employees. Tencent has cut over 10,000 workers in recent years.

On the job-seeker side, over 73 percent of 2024 graduates now prefer government or state-owned enterprise roles over private tech firms — a dramatic cultural reversal from the 996 work-culture days when private tech was the aspirational employer.

But the AI hiring side of China tells a completely different story. AI talent demand outpaced supply at a 3-to-1 ratio in Q1 2025. AI sector job seekers surged 33.4 percent in the first week after Chinese New Year 2025. Algorithm engineer postings grew 46.8 percent year-over-year and machine learning roles grew 40.1 percent year-over-year in February 2025. Average AI role salaries exceed 20,000 yuan per month. DeepSeek's January 2026 model releases triggered a nationwide AI hiring scramble across Alibaba, Tencent, ByteDance, Huawei, and hundreds of smaller firms. DeepSeek itself pays 14-month salaries, with deep learning researchers earning up to 1.54 million yuan per year — approximately $211,000 US dollars. As of March 2026, DeepSeek posted 17 new agentic AI job openings.

Major individual hiring campaigns include Shein recruiting 1,000 engineers for real-time trend prediction algorithms, Pony.ai expanding its robotaxi fleet to 15 cities seeking sensor fusion specialists, and DeepRoute.ai recruiting 500 or more engineers for Level 5 autonomous trucking. LinkedIn shows over 43,000 software developer positions listed for China.

## 6. Firmware and Embedded Systems: A Distinct Trajectory

Firmware and embedded engineering is moving in a fundamentally different direction from general software development — characterized by persistent and worsening talent shortage rather than the oversupply seen in web and app development.

In the USA, there are approximately 99,014 firmware engineers and 171,613 embedded software engineers employed. Projected demand growth is 21 percent from 2018 to 2028. Salary ranges run from $105,000 to $165,651 per year for embedded software, with an average of $92,522 for firmware engineers according to PayScale's 2026 data. IoT and embedded roles on ZipRecruiter are advertised between $111,000 and $215,000.

In Europe, the EU embedded systems market is part of a global embedded systems market projected to reach $137 billion by 2027 at a 6.5 percent compound annual growth rate. Semiconductors dominate EU embedded job postings at 33 percent of all openings, with automotive representing 26 percent. The average remote embedded software engineer salary in Europe is €52,723 per year. In Germany specifically, embedded software engineers average €66,500 per year.

The shortage numbers are striking. 80 percent of embedded engineering job postings go unfilled for months or even years, according to RunTime Recruitment. 65 percent of the 200 leading embedded systems companies surveyed struggle to fill IoT, microcontroller, and embedded software roles. 30 percent of organizations in 2025 reported a shortage of embedded security architects specifically.

Why does embedded diverge so sharply from general software? Several reasons. AI coding tools provide limited help for embedded work — it requires deep hardware knowledge, C and C++ on constrained systems, real-time operating systems, bootloaders, and physical debugging with oscilloscopes and logic analyzers. The graduate pipeline is thin because students gravitate toward higher-visibility web and mobile development. And demand is rising from multiple booming sectors simultaneously: electric vehicles, advanced driver assistance systems, autonomous vehicles, Industry 4.0 factory automation, medical IoT, smart home devices, and edge AI all need embedded and firmware talent. The Rust programming language's growing adoption in firmware is also creating an additional skills gap.

The most in-demand skills in firmware and embedded for 2025 are C and C++ — still dominant — real-time operating systems like FreeRTOS, Zephyr, and QNX, hardware abstraction layers and device drivers, bootloaders, secure boot and over-the-air firmware update mechanisms, edge AI integration with frameworks like TensorFlow Lite and ONNX on microcontrollers, communication protocols including CAN, I2C, SPI, UART, BLE, and MQTT, Rust, and functional safety standards including ISO 26262, IEC 61508, and FDA frameworks for medical devices.

## 7. Key Drivers Summary

The single most significant factor across all regions is AI's impact on hiring. AI is the primary structural force suppressing junior and entry-level hiring globally. AI coding tools reduce the per-developer cost of generalist tasks. Simultaneously, AI is creating entirely new engineering roles — machine learning engineer, AI infrastructure, LLM fine-tuning — that command premium salaries. By early 2026, 20.4 percent of confirmed layoffs cite AI or automation as a direct cause, up from less than 8 percent in 2025.

Economic conditions vary by region. In the USA, conditions are cautious but recovering; Big Tech is hiring below pandemic peaks but recovering from the 2023 trough; Federal Reserve rate environment and macro uncertainty keep hiring selective. In Europe, hiring activity is broadly weak; the UK outperforms the continent; Germany is hit by a manufacturing sector slowdown compounding IT budget pressure. In China, domestic economic slowdown plus the government's 2021 through 2023 crackdown on private tech suppressed legacy hiring, while AI strategy investments are bucking that trend.

Structural factors include the post-pandemic hiring correction — the 2021 through 2022 overhiring era created a prolonged correction through 2024 and 2025. Remote work normalization means US developers command premium rates while European and Chinese talent is increasingly accessible across borders. And strategic semiconductor investments — the US CHIPS Act, the EU Chips Act, and China's national AI and semiconductor plans — are creating specialized embedded and hardware hiring booms entirely distinct from the software generalist market trends.

---

## 8. Outlook for Early 2026

For the United States: the direction is cautiously improving overall, but new postings are down 15 percent year-to-date. Entry-level is in severe contraction. Senior roles are stable with AI specializations booming. Firmware and embedded is growing with a significant talent shortage.

For the United Kingdom: flat to slight growth with vacancies up 0.49 percent year-over-year. Entry-level is declining sharply. Senior and specialist roles are stable to growing. Firmware and embedded talent is scarce.

For the EU broadly, particularly Germany and France: Germany's postings are declining while France's are growing; both face persistent senior talent gaps. Entry-level is down 35 to 40 percent. Firmware and embedded faces a critical shortage with 80 percent of postings unfilled.

For China: the market is bifurcated. Legacy Big Tech roles are shrinking as large platforms plateau. AI specialists are in extreme demand at a 3-to-1 demand-to-supply ratio. The embedded and firmware market is growing alongside the electric vehicle and autonomous vehicle sectors.
